Frequently Asked Questions

Is the 2026 BMW X5 faster than the Lexus GX?

Yes — by a significant margin. The BMW X5 xDrive40i reaches 60 mph in 5.1 seconds. The Lexus GX 550 takes 6.5 seconds. Despite the GX having higher peak torque on paper, the X5's turbocharged inline-6 delivers power more efficiently, resulting in a 1.4-second advantage in real-world acceleration.

Which gets better gas mileage — the BMW X5 or Lexus GX?

The BMW X5 is significantly more fuel efficient. The X5 xDrive40i is EPA-rated at 23 city / 27 highway MPG. The Lexus GX 550 gets 17 city / 22 highway MPG. That's a 6 MPG city advantage for the X5 — translating to several hundred dollars in annual fuel savings.

Which has more cargo space — the BMW X5 or Lexus GX?

The BMW X5 has significantly more cargo space — 72.3 cubic feet with the rear seats folded versus 49.3 cubic feet in the GX. Behind the second row with seats up, the X5 also leads with 33.9 cu ft vs just 20.1 cu ft in the GX. For everyday use, the X5's cargo advantage is substantial.

Does the Lexus GX tow more than the BMW X5?

Yes — the Lexus GX has a higher towing rating at 9,063 lbs versus 7,200 lbs for the BMW X5. If towing a large boat, camper, or trailer regularly is your primary requirement, the GX has an advantage. For occasional towing, the X5's 7,200 lb rating handles most real-world needs comfortably.

Which is a better value — the BMW X5 or Lexus GX?

The BMW X5 starts at $67,300 — slightly below the Lexus GX's $67,735 base price — and includes 3 years / 36,000 miles of complimentary scheduled maintenance. Lexus covers only two service visits in the first year. Combined with the X5's fuel savings, the BMW delivers more total value over a typical ownership period.
