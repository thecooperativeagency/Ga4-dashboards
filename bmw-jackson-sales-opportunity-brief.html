BMW of Jackson — Where We Can Help

Internal strategy brief · cleaned prospect view · improvement opportunities and consulting angles

Strategic Read

BMW of Jackson looks like a strong example of a store where the opportunity is not just more leads. After cleaning the prospect export, the store still shows a heavy internet lead mix, but the sold output from internet prospects is far weaker than from walk-in and store-generated business. That creates a clear consulting wedge: internet lead handling, source quality control, and CRM cleanup.

Data note: the original prospect export had repeated client records. A first-pass dedupe reduced the file from 441 rows to 346 cleaned prospect records. The analysis below uses the cleaned counts.

Consulting Angles

  • Internet lead process audit: first-response, call cadence, and appointment conversion.
  • Lead-quality cleanup: separate bad/duplicate/noisy prospects from workable demand.
  • Source-quality analysis: identify which sources are productive vs cluttered.
  • Rep performance breakdown: load, open pipeline, and sold output by salesperson.
  • CRM hygiene reset: build a cleaner management reporting layer.

What This Lets You Say

  • "The store seems to have enough internet opportunity already — the bigger issue is converting it."
  • "Walk-in and store-driven business appears to close much better than internet traffic."
  • "We can help management see which lead sources are real opportunities and which are mostly noise."
  • "The CRM export needed cleanup, but even after cleaning it, the same core internet-conversion story remains."

Recommended Improvement Playbook

  1. Start with an internet lead audit. That is where the biggest volume and biggest weakness both sit.
  2. Build a source-quality scorecard. Management should review cleaned source volume vs sold yield weekly.
  3. Separate usable prospects from noisy prospects faster. That will improve focus and manager visibility.
  4. Create rep-level cleaned conversion views. The store needs clearer accountability around who is converting what.
  5. Use this cleaned month as the baseline. Future gains can be measured against it.

Data Needed to Copy This for Each Dealership

  • Store identification: dealership name, month/date range, rooftop/store group context
  • Row-level prospect export: client name, prospect type, status, source, salesperson, created date, last activity
  • Optional but useful: trade/equity fields, manager assignment, activity notes, appointment fields if available
  • Best practice: use the same export structure so dedupe + source-quality logic can be reused consistently
