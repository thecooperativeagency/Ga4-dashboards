🚨 Executive Summary

GA4 data for the last 30 days shows Google Paid and Facebook Paid campaigns bouncing at 80–87% on model pages while organic traffic bounces at 6–9% on the same pages. The pages are not broken — the campaign strategy is routing the wrong audience to the wrong page. Additionally, /service/schedule-service shows a 93.9% bounce rate from organic search, which is likely a broken or non-functional scheduling form. That issue is flagged separately — it is not a J&L issue and should be routed to the website team.

📌 Note: /service/schedule-service — Not a J&L Issue

The 93.9% bounce rate on this page is driven almost entirely by organic search traffic (31 of 33 sessions). This is likely a broken or non-loading scheduling form — not a paid media campaign issue. This should be escalated to the website/platform team for investigation. Do not include this in any J&L performance discussions.

📋 Root Cause

  • Google Paid and Facebook Paid campaigns are routing traffic to model overview pages (new-x3, new-x5, new-x7, new-5-series) instead of dedicated landing pages with specific offers
  • Organic users arrive with high purchase intent (they searched for a specific model) and engage — 6–9% bounce rate
  • Paid users arrive with variable intent (they responded to an ad) and bounce when they don't see an immediate offer, payment, or reason to stay — 80–87% bounce rate
  • The pages are not broken. The campaign targeting and landing page strategy needs adjustment
  • Facebook paid campaigns may be the most acute issue — social ad audiences have even lower purchase intent than paid search, yet they're landing on the same bare model pages

✅ Recommended Actions for J&L

  1. Build dedicated VLPs (Vehicle Landing Pages) for X3 and X5 paid campaigns — these are the two highest-volume paid pages on the site. Include current payment/lease offer, available inventory count, and a single strong CTA. The model overview pages are not converting paid traffic at any meaningful rate.
  2. Review Facebook ad creative for X3 campaigns — ensure the specific model shown in the ad matches what's on the landing page. Generic BMW ads routing to /cars/new-x3 create a disconnect. Consider model-specific creative per campaign.
  3. Review Google Ads Quality Scores on X3 and X5 keywords — an 80%+ bounce rate will lower Quality Score over time, which raises CPC and reduces ad rank. Landing page relevance is now a cost-per-click issue, not just a conversion issue.
  4. Consider VLA (Vehicle Listing Ads) for model-specific traffic — VLAs surface specific inventory with price and photo directly in the ad unit, which pre-qualifies intent before the click. This has been proven effective for this store in prior strategy work.
  5. Escalate /service/schedule-service to the website team (not J&L) — this is a separate platform/form issue and should not be attributed to paid media performance.
