Audi Baton Rouge — Where We Can Help

Internal strategy brief · month-to-date sales snapshot · improvement opportunities and consulting angles

Strategic Read

Audi Baton Rouge looks like a store where the opportunity is not simply more leads — it is getting more out of the leads already coming in. With 601 leads MTD and only 49 showroom visits logged, the biggest likely leverage point is internet lead conversion into appointments and store traffic. The low/unclear showroom progression data also makes this a strong candidate for a CRM discipline and sales-process visibility project.

Consulting Angles

  • Internet lead process audit: review how web leads are worked and where they stall.
  • Appointment conversion cleanup: understand why so few scheduled appointments are showing up against total lead volume.
  • CRM discipline reset: tighten showroom, demo, and desk stage usage so reporting becomes meaningful.
  • Manager scorecard buildout: give leadership a clean weekly conversion view.
  • Rep efficiency breakdown: identify who is producing activity vs. who is actually converting.

What This Lets You Say

  • "You already have enough lead volume to improve without buying more traffic right away."
  • "The bigger win is likely in converting internet leads into appointments and showroom visits."
  • "Right now your CRM picture makes it hard to see exactly where the leakage is. We can fix that."
  • "If we tighten stage tracking and review rhythm, management can coach to the real bottleneck instead of guessing."

Recommended Improvement Playbook

  1. Start with an internet lead audit. That is the biggest lead source and likely the fastest place to create lift.
  2. Clarify appointment definitions and workflow. The current reporting suggests either a process problem or a reporting problem.
  3. Build a clean lead → appointment → show → sold scorecard. Management needs weekly visibility.
  4. Fix showroom stage logging. Demo and desk counts at zero make the current report incomplete as an operating tool.
  5. Use this month as the before-state benchmark. Once the funnel is cleaner, future improvement becomes measurable.

Data Needed to Copy This for Each Dealership

  • Store identification: dealership name, month/date range, rooftop/store group context
  • Lead volume by source: internet leads, phone leads, walk-ins, total new leads
  • Sales funnel counts: showroom visits, demos, desked deals, sold units
  • Appointment metrics: scheduled, confirmed, completed/shown, canceled, missed/no-show
  • Activity metrics: phone in/out, email in/out, text activity if available
  • Helpful extras: salesperson-level conversion, lead source conversion, response times, contact rate, show rate, sold rate, gross per unit
