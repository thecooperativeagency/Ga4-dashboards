Strategic Read

The store likely does not have a top-of-funnel problem. It appears to have a visibility, process, and accountability problem. That is good news for us, because those are problems we can help solve. With 914 leads, 346 showroom visits, and 116 sold MTD, the bigger opportunity is to help management understand where the pipeline is leaking and then give them a cleaner operating rhythm to improve lead handling, appointments, showroom progression, and conversion.

Consulting Angles

  • Sales process audit: review how the store handles internet leads, phones, appointments, and showroom traffic.
  • CRM discipline cleanup: standardize stage definitions so reports actually tell the truth.
  • Manager scorecard buildout: give leadership a weekly operating dashboard they can coach from.
  • Lead conversion analysis: identify where leads are stalling between first response, appointment, show, and sold.
  • Rep performance breakdown: show where variance by salesperson or source is hurting output.

What This Lets You Say

  • "You have enough lead volume — the bigger opportunity is getting more out of the volume you already have."
  • "Before we talk about buying more leads, let’s tighten the conversion path on the leads already coming in."
  • "Your reporting makes it hard to see where deals are actually being lost. We can clean that up."
  • "The store likely has hidden opportunity inside appointments, follow-up, and showroom progression."

Recommended Improvement Playbook

  1. Start with a 30-day baseline audit. Confirm definitions for leads, appointments, showroom visits, demos, desked deals, and sold units.
  2. Map the real lead funnel. Build one clean view from lead → contact → appointment → show → demo → desk → sold.
  3. Break out internet leads separately. Since that is the biggest lead source, it deserves its own conversion view.
  4. Establish manager review rhythm. Weekly scorecard review with clear ownership by desk/sales managers.
  5. Coach to conversion leakage. Once the data is clean, focus on the stage with the biggest drop-off.

Data Needed to Copy This for Each Dealership

  • Store identification: dealership name, month/date range, rooftop/store group context
  • Lead volume by source: internet leads, phone leads, walk-ins, total new leads
  • Sales funnel counts: showroom visits, demos, desked deals, sold units
  • Appointment metrics: scheduled, confirmed, completed/shown, canceled, missed/no-show
  • Activity metrics: phone in/out, email in/out, text activity if available
  • Optional but very helpful: salesperson-by-salesperson conversion data, lead source conversion, manager-level accountability metrics, gross/profit by unit, days to first response, contact rate, appointment-set rate, show rate
  • Best source format: one clean screenshot/export per store from the same reporting view so the pages are apples-to-apples
