Management Readout

Audi Baton Rouge appears to have healthy lead volume and strong visible activity, but the current month-to-date sales snapshot suggests the bigger opportunity is in store conversion and stage visibility. The internet lead flow is there, outbound activity is high, and sold volume exists, but the appointment and showroom progression numbers do not fully reconcile. Management would likely benefit most from tightening reporting consistency and identifying where internet leads are failing to turn into in-store opportunities.

Current Strengths

  • Lead volume is healthy. 601 MTD leads is enough activity to work with, especially at this rooftop scale.
  • Internet is the main lever. 78.0% of all leads are internet leads, so improving the internet process could have outsized impact.
  • The team is active. Over 1,000 outbound calls/emails means there is visible effort in the system.

Near-Term Opportunities

  • Appointment reporting needs cleanup. 7 scheduled, 6 confirmed, 18 complete, 36 canceled, and 4 missed does not tell a clean story.
  • Showroom stage visibility is weak. 49 visits with 0 demos and 0 desked deals almost certainly means incomplete logging.
  • Management likely needs a clearer scorecard. Right now the store may be working hard without a clean way to see where conversion is leaking.

Recommended Management Actions

  1. Confirm stage definitions with managers and the sales team. Showroom visits, demos, desked deals, appointments, and sold units all need to be logged consistently.
  2. Review internet lead handling first. That is where the largest share of opportunity sits and likely where the biggest lift can happen.
  3. Build a cleaner weekly scorecard. Management should have one simple lead → appointment → show → sold view to coach from.
  4. Use this month as the baseline. Once logging is tighter, this becomes a useful before/after benchmark.
