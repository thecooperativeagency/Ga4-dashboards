First Read

BMW of Jackson appears to have a very large internet lead pool, but the export suggests the store converts far better in walk-in, repeat, referral, and desk-driven deal flow than it does from raw internet opportunity. That likely means the biggest opportunity is not more internet traffic — it is cleaner internet lead handling, better qualification, and less CRM clutter from duplicates and low-quality leads.

Main Opportunity Angles

  • Internet conversion problem: the biggest story in this export.
  • Database clutter problem: duplicates and bad leads are too large a share to ignore.
  • Lead source quality problem: sources need ranking by real productivity, not just volume.
  • Rep workflow problem: top reps may be overloaded or following uneven process.

What This Lets You Say

  • "BMW Jackson doesn’t appear to need more raw internet volume first — it needs to convert the volume it already has better."
  • "Walk-in and store-driven business appears to close much better than internet business."
  • "Your CRM dataset is carrying a lot of duplicate and bad-lead noise, which can hide the real conversion story."
  • "There’s probably money sitting in internet follow-up, source cleanup, and rep-level process discipline."

v1 Recommended Next Steps

  1. Build a source-quality table from this CSV: source → total rows → sold → lost → duplicate → bad lead.
  2. Build a salesperson performance table: rep → total prospects → open pipeline → sold-like rows.
  3. Segment internet leads separately and inspect status distribution inside internet only.
  4. Review stale active/new prospects by created date and last activity to find follow-up leaks.
